About me

I’m a Ph.D. Candidate at the Univeristy of Florida. My research interests include the development of statistical methodology for -omics data.

Recent work has focused on RNA-seq data (transcriptomics), with methods in the areas of survival analysis, predictive modelling, and graphical models. How might gene expression profiles be used to predict patient survival times? How do gene-gene associations differ in high-risk vs. low-risk patients, and what clues do these differences leave us about the underlying disease? Future work will delve into methodology for single-cell data, which provides a much finer resolution of cellular activity.

I focus on assessing model performance through extensive simulations. Simulations force us to better understand the biological context (or data generating process) to which the methods are applied. Through simulations we can explore conditions to find out when our models work and when they fail - this is a process that has often lead me to new insights and a deeper understanding.

Featured Publications

Integrating gene regulatory pathways into differential network analysis of gene expression data

A framework for comparing gene-gene associations between two populations is proposed. Any association measure may be used - correlations, partial correlation, mutual information, etc; this flexibility is in contrast to the rigidity of existing methods. The framework makes explicit the practice of incoporating pathway information into the analysis. A simulation study explores the affect this has on performance, and we investigate what happens when pathway information is misspecified or incomplete.
Tyler Grimes, S. Steven Potter, Somnath Datta
Scientific Reports, 2019

Predicting survival times for neuroblastoma patients using RNA-seq expression profiles

In this study, expression profiles from RNA-sequencing of Neuroblastoma tumor samples are used to predict patient survival times. Several models are investigated using various annotation levels of expression profiles (genes, transcripts, and introns), and an ensemble predictor is proposed as a heuristic for combining these different profiles. The use of RNA-seq data is shown to improve accuracy in comparison to using clinical data alone for predicting overall survival times.
Tyler Grimes, Alejandro R. Walker, Susmita Datta, Somnath Datta
Biology Direct, 2018
